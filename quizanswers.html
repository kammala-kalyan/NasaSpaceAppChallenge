ANSWERS

1.What is a solar storm?

Answer: A release of energy from the Sun's surface.

Explanation: Solar storms are powerful bursts of energy caused by changes in the Sun's magnetic field. They can manifest as solar flares or coronal mass ejections (CMEs).

2.What are the two main components of a solar storm?

Answer: Solar flares and CMEs.

Explanation: Solar storms primarily consist of solar flares, which are bright flashes of light, and coronal mass ejections (CMEs), which are large clouds of solar plasma and magnetic fields.

3.How can solar storms affect Earth?

Answer: They can disrupt power grids and satellite communications

Explanation: Solar storms can create geomagnetic storms that disrupt electrical systems, GPS, and satellite communications, affecting various technologies we rely on daily.

4.What was the highest geomagnetic storm rating in May 2024?

Answer: G5.

Explanation: The May 2024 solar storm reached a G5 rating, which is the highest level on the geomagnetic storm scale and can cause significant disruptions to technology on Earth.

5.What do solar flares release?

Answer: Light and energy.

Explanation: Solar flares emit high amounts of light and energy, including radiation that can affect satellites and communication systems on Earth.

6.How do CMEs (Coronal Mass Ejections) impact satellites?

Answer: They can interfere with communication signals.

Explanation: CMEs can distort or disrupt the signals that satellites use for communication, leading to issues like GPS malfunctions and interrupted internet services.

7.Where are auroras usually visible during solar storms?

Answer: Near the poles.

Explanation: Auroras typically occur near the poles, but during strong solar storms, they can be seen at lower latitudes, making them visible in areas further from the poles than usual.

8.How did the May 2024 solar storm affect farmers?

Answer: It disrupted satellite systems used for irrigation planning.

Explanation: Many modern farming techniques rely on satellite systems for things like weather predictions and irrigation. The disruptions caused by the May 2024 solar storm impacted farmers' ability to manage their crops effectively.
